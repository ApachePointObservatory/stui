TUI:Scripts:Built In Scripts:NICFPS:Data Cube

The NICFPS:Data Cube script allows you to take a sequence of exposures over a range of wavelengths. You specify:

  • The number of exposures per etalon z spacing
  • The initial etalon z spacing
  • The approximate final etalon z spacing. (The spacing is changed by strictly equal intervals, so the final spacing may be slightly different from that requested.)
  • The number of etalon z spacings at which to expose. 2 is the smallest allowed value and will expose at just the beginning and ending spacings.

The resulting Δz (spacing interval) is displayed. Etalon z spacings are traversed in two interleaved passes:

  1. beg z, beg z + 2 Δz, beg z + 4 Δz, ...
  2. beg z + Δz, beg z + 3 Δz...

Warning: this script does not restore the etalon z spacing to its original value when done. So NICFPS will almost certainly have a different spacing after you run this script.
